Earnings season did what earnings season does: it turned the AI debate from theory into line items. Alphabet posted a quarter strong enough to beat on nearly every metric and still sent its own stock lower, because the number investors fixated on was the one with the most zeros—capex. Nvidia, meanwhile, showed how it keeps the flywheel spinning, disclosing a stake in one of the cloud firms that buys its chips. On the positioning side, two quieter deals showed where GNSS actually earns its keep: charging trucks on European highways, and steering tractors down a row to within an inch.

## Tech News

### Alphabet Beats on Everything—Then Sinks on a $205 Billion Capex Number

**Alphabet** opened Big Tech's earnings season Wednesday with **$119.8 billion** in Q2 revenue, up **24%** year over year, and a Google Cloud business that surged **82%** to **$24.8 billion** on AI-infrastructure demand—with cloud backlog swelling to **$514 billion**, per CNBC and Yahoo Finance. On almost every line the quarter beat, and net income came in above expectations.

Then the stock fell in extended trading, because Alphabet raised its **2026 capital-expenditure** guidance to a range topping out near **$205 billion**—up sharply from the **$180–190 billion** it guided in April, per Seeking Alpha. Q2 capex alone was **$44.9 billion**, more than double a year earlier. It is the same tension that has hung over the sector all month, now stated in a single company's guidance: the cloud growth is real, the demand is real, and the spending required to serve it keeps climbing faster than investors are comfortable underwriting.

### Nvidia Reveals a 9.3% Stake in Neocloud Nebius, and the Stock Jumps 19%

**Nvidia** disclosed a **9.3%** beneficial ownership stake in **Nebius**, the Amsterdam-based AI-cloud infrastructure firm, in a Schedule 13G filing—roughly **22.25 million** shares once a pre-funded warrant is counted, tracing back to a **$2 billion** investment Nvidia made in March, per CNBC and The Motley Fool. Nebius shares jumped nearly **19%** to lead the Nasdaq on the news.

The disclosure is a clean look at how Nvidia keeps its own demand primed: it sells GPUs to the neoclouds, then takes equity in the neoclouds that buy them. Nebius is a "neocloud"—a newer class of provider renting out GPU capacity to companies that can't get it fast enough from the hyperscalers—and Nvidia's backing gives it both capital and a credibility stamp. Critics call the pattern circular; Nvidia calls it investing in the ecosystem. Either way, it is why the company's gravitational pull over the AI trade keeps growing.

### Apple Plans to Overhaul Its Entire Mac Line for the AI Era

**Apple** is preparing to refresh **every Mac it sells**—MacBook Air, iMac, MacBook Pro, Mac mini, Mac Studio and a new model reported as "Neo"—across this fall and into next year, seeking to capture demand for powerful local machines driven by the AI boom, per Bloomberg's Mark Gurman. The push pairs next-generation Apple silicon with on-device AI features that benefit from more memory and compute.

The strategy is a bet that not all of AI moves to the cloud. While Alphabet and Nvidia spend hundreds of billions building centralized capacity, Apple is wagering that a meaningful slice of inference stays on the desk—private, fast, and paid for once at purchase rather than metered by the token. A full-lineup refresh is also simply good hardware timing: the Mac install base is due, and "AI PC" is the label that moves upgrades this cycle.

## GNSS News

### NORBIT Lands a NOK 325 Million Repeat Order for GNSS Tolling Units

Norway's **NORBIT** signed a new agreement worth roughly **NOK 325 million** (about **$32 million**) to supply **GNSS On-Board Units** to **Toll4Europe**, a leading European Electronic Toll Service (EETS) provider, per NORBIT's GlobeNewswire release. Deliveries begin toward the end of Q4 2026, with the bulk landing in 2027—and the company framed it as a repeat order, following a **NOK 155 million** deal with the same customer in May.

The deal is a reminder that one of GNSS's largest commercial markets is not phones or tractors but trucks paying to use the road. Satellite-based tolling lets operators charge by distance and route across borders without roadside gantries, and the On-Board Unit is the meter. A second nine-figure order inside three months signals that Europe's shift to GNSS free-flow tolling is moving from pilots to fleet-scale rollout—exactly the kind of unglamorous, high-volume application that pays the industry's bills.

### Tersus GNSS Launches the AG993 Autosteer Kit With L-Band Correction Fallback

**Tersus GNSS** launched the **AG993**, a modular autosteer retrofit kit that targets better-than-**2.5 cm** accuracy and works across a 0.2–30 km/h speed range, pairing conventional RTK with the company's proprietary **TAP** L-band satellite-correction service, per Inside GNSS and GPS World. The headline feature is **TAPFill**: if RTK correction drops out, the system automatically switches to TAP—delivered by satellite rather than a base station or cellular link—while keeping the solution aligned to the RTK reference frame so guidance never breaks.

That fallback is the point. RTK gives centimeter precision but depends on a nearby base or a live cellular connection, both of which fail exactly where a lot of farming happens—remote fields, dead zones, the far corner of the property. By backstopping RTK with an L-band signal that reaches anywhere the sky is visible, Tersus is selling continuity, not just accuracy. The kit fits more than **90%** of agricultural vehicles and supports GPS, Galileo, GLONASS, BeiDou, QZSS and SBAS, at an indicative **$4,000–8,000**.

## Key Takeaways

- **Alphabet's quarter reframed the AI debate as a capex problem**: A 24% revenue beat and 82% Cloud growth weren't enough to hold the stock, which fell after the company lifted 2026 capex guidance toward $205 billion—proof that investors now grade Big Tech on spending discipline as much as growth.
- **Nvidia keeps priming its own demand**: Disclosing a 9.3% stake in neocloud Nebius sent that stock up 19% and illustrated the loop powering the AI trade—Nvidia sells GPUs to the clouds, then takes equity in them—while Apple bet the other way, overhauling its whole Mac line for on-device AI.
- **GNSS earns its keep in tolling booths and tractor cabs**: NORBIT's second nine-figure On-Board Unit order in three months shows satellite tolling scaling across Europe, while Tersus's AG993 answers RTK's weakness—dropped corrections—with automatic L-band fallback that keeps autosteer running where cellular can't reach.
