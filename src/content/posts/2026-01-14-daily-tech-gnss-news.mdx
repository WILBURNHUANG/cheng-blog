## Tech News

### US Approves Nvidia H200 Chip Exports to China with Conditions

The Commerce Department formally approved exports of Nvidia's H200 AI chips to China on January 14, 2026, marking a significant shift in US semiconductor policy. Export applications will now be evaluated on a **case-by-case basis** rather than starting from a presumption of denial.

Chinese technology companies have placed orders for more than **2 million H200 chips** priced at approximately **$27,000 each**, far exceeding Nvidia's current inventory of 700,000 units. The chips must pass third-party lab testing to confirm their AI capabilities before shipment, and China cannot receive more than **50% of total chips sold** to American customers.

The approval follows months of lobbying by Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang. President Trump announced last month that advanced chips could be sold to "approved customers" in China for a **25% government surcharge**. Notably, Nvidia's more sophisticated Blackwell processor and upcoming Rubin model are not part of the deal. Democratic senators have objected, warning the chips could aid China's military and cyberattack capabilities.

## GNSS News

### Trimble Powers Lucid Gravity with Lane-Level Positioning

Trimble announced its positioning technology will be standard on all **Lucid Gravity** electric SUVs starting in late January 2026, with existing vehicles receiving the capability via over-the-air update. The integration combines Trimble RTX corrections with ProPoint Go inertial technology for centimeter-level accuracy.

The system fuses satellite correction data with six-axis inertial sensors to maintain precise positioning in challenging environments like tunnels, parking garages, and urban canyons where traditional GNSS signals struggle. Trimble reports the solution can reduce positional error to **a few centimeters**, enabling the vehicle to confirm lane occupancy and support higher-confidence automated driving features.

This marks another milestone in Trimble's push into automotive applications, as precision positioning becomes essential for advanced driver assistance systems and eventual autonomous driving.

### ANELLO Aerial INS Launches for Drone Navigation

ANELLO Photonics unveiled its Aerial INS at CES 2026, a compact inertial navigation system built around the company's **Silicon Photonics Optical Gyroscope (SiPhOG)** technology. The system integrates multi-band GNSS receivers for high-performance navigation in aerial applications.

The optical gyroscope technology offers advantages over traditional MEMS sensors in terms of bias stability and noise performance, making it particularly suited for applications requiring navigation continuity during GNSS outages or in GPS-denied environments. The launch positions ANELLO as a key player in the growing market for drone navigation in surveying, mapping, and inspection applications.
