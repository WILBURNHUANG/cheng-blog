AI governance dominated the conversation at the IMF Spring Meetings today, while chipmakers continued to ride surging AI infrastructure demand. In positioning, CHC Navigation rolled out a broad refresh of its RTK receiver lineup.

## Tech News

### IMF and Regulators Sound Alarm on AI Cyber Risk

Governments must "stay at the frontier" of mounting AI threats, a top IMF official warned at the fund's Spring Meetings, where fears about the destructive potential of Anthropic's new **Mythos** model dominated side conversations. Bloomberg reports the IMF is pushing for proactive oversight as AI-enabled cyberattacks grow more sophisticated.

Separately, the US Treasury Department's technology team is seeking access to **Mythos** so it can hunt for vulnerabilities before broader release. ECB President Christine Lagarde also publicly backed Anthropic's staged rollout approach, under which Apple and Amazon received early access to test the model ahead of any wider launch.

### Anthropic Revenue Run Rate Tops $30 Billion

Anthropic's annualized revenue has climbed to roughly **$30 billion**, up from **$9 billion** at the end of 2025, according to Bloomberg. The company has locked in about **3.5 gigawatts** of compute capacity tied to Google's TPUs via an expanded Broadcom deal, with Broadcom supplying **1 gigawatt** in 2026 and scaling past **3 gigawatts** in 2027.

Anthropic also agreed this week to rent additional AI capacity from CoreWeave to power Claude, underlining how aggressively frontier labs are locking up multi-year compute supply.

### TSMC Rides AI Demand to Record Quarter

Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing reported Q1 2026 revenue of roughly **$36 billion**, up about **35%** year over year, as AI chip demand showed no signs of cooling. March revenue alone surged **45%**, Bloomberg reports, helping push Taiwan's Taiex Index to a record intraday high. TSMC's full earnings call is scheduled for April 16.

### Additional Headlines

- **Canadian AI minister praises Anthropic**: Ottawa lauded the company's staged "Mythos approach" as a model for responsible frontier AI rollout.
- **OpenAI pitches compute edge**: OpenAI told investors it holds a computing advantage over Anthropic as the two race to scale inference capacity.
- **Arctic connectivity play**: A Quebec firm that helped link NASA's Artemis astronauts is adapting its satellite comms tech to close surveillance and connectivity gaps across Canada's Arctic.

## GNSS News

### CHC Navigation Overhauls RTK Receiver Lineup

CHC Navigation announced major updates to its **i93**, **i85**, and **i76** GNSS receivers along with the **iBase** professional base station, GPS World reports. The refreshed receivers integrate the company's **StellaX GNSS chip** with multi-frequency signal tracking and enhanced anti-interference mitigation, targeting more stable RTK performance in complex terrain and areas with partial satellite visibility.

To reduce reliance on cellular networks and local radio links in the field, the new i93 and i85 now ship with a one-year subscription to CHC's **PointSky** correction service — a notable shift toward PPP-RTK style delivery as surveyors increasingly work in connectivity-poor environments. The iBase update rounds out the stack for crews running their own reference infrastructure.

### Hybrid RTK Gains Traction for IoT

A GPS World feature this week highlights **Hybrid RTK** as a scalable path to high-precision positioning for IoT-scale deployments, blending network RTK with lighter-weight correction models to keep bandwidth and power budgets manageable. The piece underscores how the precision positioning market is bifurcating between survey-grade hardware and mass-market IoT endpoints.
